📚 Workshops & Events

Attend regular workshops and events covering topics essential to student success and personal development.

Recent & Upcoming Topics:

  • Academic Success: Study strategies, time management, and exam preparation
  • Financial Literacy: Understanding loans, budgeting, and financial aid
  • Career Exploration: Resume writing, interview skills, and networking
  • Mental Health & Wellness: Stress management and wellness resources
  • Cultural & Social Events: Community building and celebration

Schedule: Workshops held monthly throughout the semester. Check our email newsletter or visit our office for current dates and times.

🤝 Peer Support Groups

Join peer-led support groups to build community with other first-generation students and share experiences in a safe, welcoming environment.

Group Options:

  • First-Year Transition Group: For newly admitted first-generation students
  • General Peer Support Circle: Open to all first-generation students
  • Identity-Specific Groups: Sessions celebrating diverse intersectional identities

Meeting Schedule: Weekly sessions throughout the semester. Flexible timing to accommodate student schedules.

💼 Career Development Services

Access comprehensive career resources, networking opportunities, and professional development support to prepare for your future success.

Services Include:

  • One-on-one career counseling and resume reviews
  • Interview preparation and mock interviews
  • Networking events and alumni connections
  • Internship and job search assistance
  • LinkedIn profile development
  • Professional development workshops

Getting Started: Schedule a career consultation by emailing firstgen@babson.edu.

📖 Resource Center

Access our comprehensive online and in-person resource library designed specifically for first-generation students.

Available Resources:

  • Study guides and academic success tips
  • Financial aid and scholarship guides
  • Networking and career development materials
  • Mental health and wellness resources
  • Campus navigation guides
  • Peer-written letters from successful first-generation students

Access: Visit our office or email us to receive digital copies of our resource guides.
